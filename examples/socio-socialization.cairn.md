# Sociological: Primary and Secondary Socialization

Cairn description of socialization processes through which individuals internalize the norms, values, and roles of their society.

## CONTEXT

- **Individual** (child or newcomer).
- **Agents**: Family (primary), institutions like school/work (secondary).
- **Society**: Norms, values, roles, culture.

## REQUIREMENTS

```
R1. Socialization is essential for social order and individual functioning. [MUST]
R2. Primary socialization occurs early and is foundational. [MUST]
R3. Secondary socialization adapts individuals to new roles/contexts. [MUST]
R4. It involves both learning and internalization. [MUST]
```

## OUTCOMES

Socialized individuals who can participate competently in society.

EMERGENT [SOCIAL: reproduction of culture and social structure]

## PROCESS — Formal

```
PROCESS Socialization (INPUT: individual entering social world; OUTPUT: socialized member)
  STATE
    norms_values [scope: individual; dir: read/write]
    roles        [scope: individual; dir: read/write]
    identity     [scope: individual; dir: read/write]

  1. SOCIALIZE Primary via family interaction and modeling. [SOCIAL, CULTURAL]
  2. ITERATE learning basic norms and roles.
  3. SOCIALIZE Secondary in new contexts (school, work, etc.). [ROLE]
  4. COALITION Align with group norms. [CULTURAL]
  5. Internalize through repeated practice and feedback.
  6. STATE UPDATE: integrated norms, roles, and self.
  7. EMERGENT [SOCIAL: competent participation and cultural continuity]
```
