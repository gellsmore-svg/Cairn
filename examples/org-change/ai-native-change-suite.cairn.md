# Organisational Change Suite: AI-Native And Trauma-Informed Change

These examples combine classic change management with newer AI-augmented,
complexity-informed, and trauma-informed organisational thinking.

## PROCESS — AI-Native Operating Model Redesign

```cairn
PROCESS AINativeOperatingModelRedesign (INPUT: fragmented_ai_adoption; OUTPUT: governed_ai_native_operating_model)
  1. Map current work, decision rights, shadow AI use, and trust fractures. [HUMAN, ASSISTED-BY: LLM]
     HUMAN_FACTORS:
       psychological_contract: employees may experience AI adoption as role threat or surveillance.
       social_role: informal AI power users may gain unacknowledged status.
     AUGMENTATION_PROCESS:
       role_complementarity: identify what AI should draft, classify, summarize, decide, or never decide.

  2. Define human, AI, and accountable-owner decision rights. [HUMAN, GATED]
     PURPOSE: prevent augmentation from becoming ambiguous accountability.
     HUMAN_RISK:
       probability: high
       impact: high
       confidence: medium
       rationale: unclear decision rights erode trust and create hidden automation bias.
     SUPPORT: publish reversible authority boundaries and escalation triggers.

  3. Pilot AI-native workflows with trust calibration and feedback loops. [ITERATIVE, ASSISTED-BY: LLM]
     HCI_TOUCHPOINT:
       phase: adaptation
       human_goal: learn when to rely on AI and when to challenge it.
     SUPPORT: show confidence, evidence, override, challenge path, and human audit outcome.

  4. Redesign roles, training, metrics, and governance. [HUMAN, MACRO]
     CHANGE_IMPACT:
       role_shift: tasks move from production to judgement, orchestration, and exception handling.
       adoption_support: psychological safety, AI literacy, peer learning, and manager coaching.
       reinforcement: measure decision quality, recovery loops, and trust calibration, not only throughput.
```

## PROCESS — Trauma-Informed ERP Rollout

```cairn
PROCESS TraumaInformedERPRollout (INPUT: new_erp_program; OUTPUT: adopted_erp_with_reduced_human_harm)
  1. Assess change history, burnout, workload, and trust before rollout. [HUMAN, READONLY]
     HUMAN_FACTORS:
       trauma_informed: assume prior change may have created threat responses, not laziness.
       cognitive_load: overloaded teams have reduced capacity for training and error recovery.

  2. Co-design rollout pacing, support, and safe escalation. [HUMAN, COLLABORATIVE]
     HUMAN_DEMAND:
       ORIENT: know what is changing, why, when, and what support exists.
       ACT: practice in realistic scenarios before high-stakes cutover.
       RECOVER: ask for help without shame or punishment.
     SUPPORT: use predictable communication, choice where possible, peer champions, and protected practice time.

  3. Run cutover with regulation and recovery loops. [SERVICE, HUMAN, GATED]
     HUMAN_RISK:
       probability: high
       impact: high
       confidence: medium
       rationale: cutover combines system novelty, time pressure, identity threat, and customer/business consequences.
     HCI_TOUCHPOINT:
       phase: recovery
       human_goal: resolve errors without panic, blame, or opaque escalation.

  4. Sustain adoption through reinforcement, listening, and workload repair. [ITERATIVE]
     CHANGE_IMPACT:
       reinforcement: remove duplicate work, repair broken reports, and publish fixes from frontline feedback.
       coalition: managers must protect learning time after go-live.
```

## PROCESS — Post-Merger Integration Of Teams And Systems

```cairn
PROCESS PostMergerIntegration (INPUT: merged_organizations; OUTPUT: integrated_operating_model)
  1. Map systems, roles, power centres, customer commitments, and identity markers. [HUMAN, READONLY]
     HUMAN_FACTORS:
       social_identity: people may protect legacy group identity when status feels threatened.
       loss_aversion: teams overweight what they lose compared with future integration benefits.

  2. Establish integration principles and decision forums. [COALITION, HUMAN]
     PURPOSE: make trade-offs explicit instead of letting informal power decide.
     SUPPORT: publish decision rights, non-negotiables, and appeal paths.

  3. Integrate process by process with customer and employee safeguards. [ITERATIVE]
     HCI_TOUCHPOINT:
       phase: handoff
       human_goal: know which legacy system, role, or policy owns the next step during transition.
     HUMAN_RISK:
       probability: high
       impact: high
       confidence: medium
       rationale: integration failures often happen at cross-system and cross-identity interfaces.

  4. Retire duplicate practices and rebuild shared rituals. [SOCIALIZE, MACRO]
     CHANGE_IMPACT:
       reinforcement: celebrate cross-legacy wins and remove status signals that keep old boundaries alive.
```

## PROCESS — Ethical AI Governance Change

```cairn
PROCESS EthicalAIGovernanceChange (INPUT: ungoverned_ai_use; OUTPUT: accountable_ai_governance)
  1. Inventory AI uses, affected groups, data flows, and decision consequences. [READONLY, ASSISTED-BY: LLM]
     HUMAN_FACTORS:
       accountability_gap: users may assume someone else validated AI output.
       behavioural_economics: convenience can overpower abstract governance risk.

  2. Classify risk and define controls. [HUMAN, GATED]
     AUGMENTATION_PROCESS:
       bias_mitigation: check affected groups, feedback routes, and contestability.
       trust_calibration: require evidence, limitations, and uncertainty displays for high-risk use.

  3. Embed governance in workflow rather than policy-only training. [UI, PROCESS]
     HCI_TOUCHPOINT:
       phase: execution
       human_goal: know what is allowed and what needs approval at the point of use.
     SUPPORT: provide lightweight request paths, examples, and reversible controls.

  4. Monitor incidents, overrides, and drift. [SERVICE, ITERATIVE]
     HUMAN_RISK:
       probability: medium
       impact: high
       confidence: medium
       rationale: AI risk changes as tools, users, incentives, and data change.
```
