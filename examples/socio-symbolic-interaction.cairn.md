# Sociological: Symbolic Interaction and Self-Formation

Cairn description of Symbolic Interactionism (Mead, Blumer) applied to identity and meaning-making in social processes.

Focuses on how individuals create and negotiate meaning through interaction, forming the self in the process.

## CONTEXT

- **Individuals** in social situations.
- **Symbols** (language, gestures, objects) that carry shared or negotiated meaning.
- **Others** (significant and generalized).
- **Self** as reflexive product of interaction.

## REQUIREMENTS

```
R1. Meaning arises from social interaction, not inherent in things. [MUST]
R2. Individuals take the role of the other to interpret situations. [MUST]
R3. The self is formed through ongoing interaction and reflection. [MUST]
R4. Social reality is constructed and maintained through these processes. [MUST]
```

## OUTCOMES

Shared meanings, coordinated action, and a stable (or evolving) sense of self and social order.

EMERGENT [SOCIAL: shared reality and identities]
